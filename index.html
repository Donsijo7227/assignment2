Synopsis

In a totalitarian future society, Winston Smith, a low-ranking civil servant in the Ministry of Truth, rebels against the omnipresent surveillance of Big Brother by daring to express his individuality.

Winston Smith lives in a society where the government monitors and controls every aspect of human life...

The novel explores themes of totalitarianism, surveillance, technological control, and the manipulation of truth...

First published in 1949 by Secker and Warburg as Smith's ninth and final book completed in his lifetime...
